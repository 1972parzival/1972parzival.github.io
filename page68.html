Conclusion

After a long secession of me and a friend brainstorming, and him doing some intital and final heat deformations caluclations and helped me determine that the cost to remove 1 Ton (1,000kg) of CO2 from the atmosphere would be roughly $513.15 at ($0.15 per Kwh) in an ideal case with about 1,908 kg of sodium bicarbonate produced as a product

Profit = (1,908*0.51) - 513.15

where $0.51 represents the cost per kg that the product could be sold for at current market price

so in a ideal case for every ton of co2 removed we profit  and about $459.93.
keep in mind this is an ideal case some things working for it is that we do have other reactants we could sell like cl2 and H2 produced from the sodium hydroxide electrolysis. 

With a more Realistic case we would expect a something larger costs leading to a net loss and no profit 

Cost per ton of CO2 =
(1,908*0.51) - (513.15 * (1/Efficiency Percent))

$309.795 per ton of CO2, assuming a more realistic Efficiency of 40% which is much higher then the market rates of $20-60 per ton of CO2

if we reintroduce the idea of cl2 and H2 produced from the sodium hydroxide electrolysis. we can add that in to the revenue side of the equation alongside the sodium bicarbonate

sodium bicarbonate kg * $/KG (1,908*0.51)+
cl2 kg * $/KG (805.4*0.35)+
H2 kg * $/KG(11.36*5)+
- ((513.15 * (1/Efficiency Percent)))
= a profit of $28.895 for every ton of CO2 removed 

this allows us about the $50 margin to be below the current costs of the lowest co2 capture method ($20 per ton) this margin can be eaten up by things like:

- a decrease the market cost due to the increase supply as are supplying to the market
- a increase in the cost of electricity  
- extra variable costs from stuff like purification of products like the h2 cl2 and the sodium bicarbonate
- a decrease in the Efficiency Percent
- transport and storage cost of products like the h2 cl2 and the sodium bicarbonate
- a large fixed equipment and facility costs spread over the lifecycle of the chambers

overall this is a interesting CO2 removal method and assuming the total extra costs do not exceed the roughly 50 dollar margin overtime, it could be THE MOST viable carbon capture method.
