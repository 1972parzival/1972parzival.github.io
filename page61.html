Overview 

I was recently approached to work on a 3D-printed product described as a “smoker’s box.” It is a small container that holds a common lighter and a separate section for cigarettes. The current model uses a simple hinge printed along the filament line, but it doesn’t provide a tight fit.

The three goals for this improved design are:

Hinge (print-in-place): Must be easily printable.

Latch system (PIP): Ensures contents are secure and won’t spill or be damaged.

Water resistance: Highly water-resistant, ideally fully waterproof.

photo of the initial model printed out

Initial model

I started printing the initial model to get a better look at it and understand its flaws:

Weak hinge: Snapped quickly during tests.

Gaps and seams: Visible around the edges.

Poor surface quality: Appears cheap when printed on the largest face, as that becomes the bottom layer.


3 versions of the latch, from old to new

Latch testing

I started out with a regular latch that had a flush, flat-face design intended to be installed with screws based on a template. I then mounted it on a flat surface and removed the side mounting holes, while also improving the latch “lip” so it required more force to unhook. Finally, I refined the latch itself so that it was fully secure, completely print-in-place, and could be easily “cracked in” when closing.

V1 notes

Both the latch and hinge function well. The next major step will be tightening the tolerances to improve water resistance. After that, finer adjustments can be made to reduce the need for supports and allow easier “cracking in” after printing. I also think it may be worth flipping the side of the lighter compartment, since most users are right-handed and would be able to light it within the container more easily.
