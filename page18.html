Spray Assist

a tool to assist in holding a spray can and getting consistent coats and easy shaking 

Version 0.1

This was a very basic model of a spray paint can mount that I found online. The original design had several issues the can was extremely unstable, and the handle was weak and broke with even minimal use. My goal is to redesign it into a more durable and user-friendly version that’s easier to print on a wider range of 3D printers.

Planned improvements include:

Adding a ring hole for convenient hanging or storage.

Designing proper grooves in the handle to improve grip and comfort.

Removing the unnecessary side logo to reduce print complexity and minimize the risk of print failure.

Implementing a secondary mounting system using nuts and bolts to improve overall stability.

Adding a circular base so the mount can stand flat and steady when not in use.

And, of course, including my own custom logo for a personal touch. :0

Version 0.2

The redesigned handle performed very well in terms of grip and control, providing much better handling of the spray can compared to the original model. However, the handle snapped under pressure, which suggests that it needs additional reinforcement to better withstand grip force. This issue should be easily fixable with internal strengthening features or thicker wall geometry in key stress areas.

The slide nut blocks looked and functioned great overall, though I discovered that one nut was modeled backward, preventing it from working properly. This will be corrected in the next revision.

Additionally, the top portion of the handle grip needs a full redesign, as the current print had to be canceled mid-process to avoid printing the overly complex upper sections inherited from the original model. Simplifying this geometry will improve both print reliability and ergonomic consistency in future iterations.

Version 0.3


Most of the issues identified in the 0.2 notes were addressed in this iteration.

Main Changes:

Properly reinforced the handle to improve strength and prevent snapping.

Added custom logo to the body.

Fixed the reversed nut orientation on one side.

Notes:

The handle reinforcement was successful; it’s now far sturdier. However, the increased material has slightly reduced movement clearance, making it more difficult to adjust in tight spaces.

The logo printed cleanly but is slightly off-center — a minor alignment issue to fix in the next version.

Overall, this version serves as a solid base model, and work will now shift to developing the connector component.

Version 0.4

Began work on the connector system, which will attach the base to the holder using the previously discussed nut-and-bolt mounting setup. This version is a rough prototype aimed at testing alignment and fit rather than overall durability.

Notes:

Left side fit: Alignment and hole spacing are good fits cleanly with minimal adjustment needed.

Right side: Misaligned the section appears shifted too far from center, preventing the bolt from passing through properly.

The main column sits too close to the spray can, causing unwanted bending under pressure rather than a flush, secure fit.

Next version will adjust centerline spacing and column offset to achieve better symmetry and improve load distribution.

version 0.5

Improvements Made:

Bolt alignment issue fixed both sides now line up correctly with the nut holes.

Added a proper base that fits neatly and sits flush with the rest of the model.

Main column repositioned so that it is flush with the side of the spray paint can, eliminating previous bending issues.

This version now provides a much more stable and aligned connector system, creating a solid foundation for further refinements.

version 0.6 and 0.7

Objective:
Working on the holder system, designed to securely attach to the connector using the nut-and-bolt system.

New Additions:

Added a proper bottom to the holder, allowing it to securely hold the spray can and sit flat on a surface.

Extended the length of the holder to fully incorporate the required nut for attachment.

Observed Issues:

The area between the base and connector repeatedly breaks under stress testing.

There is excessive flex at the bottom of the connector, reducing overall stability.

Next Steps:

Reinforce the base-to-connector joint to handle stress more reliably.

Reduce flex by adjusting geometry or adding internal supports in the connector bottom.

version 0.8 and 0.9

Changes Made:

Base-to-connector joint: Increased the surface area of the base by 269.25% to reduce the likelihood of breakage under stress testing.

Bottom of connector: Added an additional screw and corresponding hole to reduce flex and improve stability.

Notes:

These are the only changes in this iteration.

No additional photos were included, as the overall appearance remains largely unchanged from versions 0.6 and 0.7.

Two timelapses were captured, but they are almost identical to previous versions.

Version 1.0

Problem:
The handle mechanism on the base has proven to be fundamentally flawed. Reinforcements made in v0.3 reduced its rotation, making the current design inefficient and difficult to operate.

Proposed Solution:

Implement a basic gear system so that a small, high-force movement of the handle translates into a larger, lower-force movement in the pressing area.

This approach will also allow for finer user control over the amount of paint dispensed.

The design and modeling will likely be done in FreeCAD to ensure precision and proper alignment.
(like we did in first 3d model)

Next Steps:

Prototype gear ratios to achieve a smooth balance between handle force and pressing motion.

Integrate the gear system into the existing base while maintaining durability.

sketch planning out new handle gear system from sketch

Version 1.1

Progress:

After weeks of testing, a reliable geared handle mechanism could not be successfully designed for the base.

Solution implemented: Increased the infill of the base to 60–70% and used a triangle infill pattern, resulting in a handle that “never” broke during stress testing.

New Issue:

The handle does not have enough forward movement, as it is being blocked by the connector piece.

Next Steps:

Adjust the connector or handle geometry to allow for full forward range of motion while maintaining the reinforced strength.

Version 1.2

Changes Made:

The connector piece has been remodeled to provide more space, allowing the handle to reach its maximum forward point.

Structural support is provided by:

Two thin cylinders on right triangles at the bottom

Two right triangles on the top

New Problems:

The wide cylinders prevent the screws that attach the connector to the base from being installed.

The top screw, which secures the holder to the connector, cannot fit due to the large cylinder diameters.

Next Steps:

Adjust the cylinder size or placement to allow screws to be installed without compromising handle clearance or structural support.

Version 1.3

Changes Made:

Reduced the width of the upper right triangles and the two cylinders to allow screw installation.

Added a square indent so that the holder and connector can lie flush and be screwed together correctly.

Outcome:

All screws can now be installed without interfering with the handle’s movement.

Structural support is maintained while ensuring proper alignment of components.

Version 1.4

Issue:

The base bolt is too tall, causing it to hit the user’s hand when gripping the handle.

Solution:

Reduce the bolt head height by 6 mm, from ~54 mm to ~48 mm.

The threaded section remains unchanged, ensuring proper fit and function.

Outcome:

User can now grip the handle comfortably without interference from the bolt.
