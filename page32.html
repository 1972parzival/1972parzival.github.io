Experimenting with Electroplating:
Part 2

Materials 

In this version, the materials are mostly the same as part one, but with two key changes. First, instead of plain water, I’m using a properly mixed Copper (II) Sulfate solution to act as the electrolyte. Second, the copper source has been upgraded from the original copper pipe to high-purity copper sticks, which should improve the reaction and the quality of the deposition.

Experiment

Watching the reaction unfold was fascinating: the copper sticks appeared to almost “decay” into tiny particles as they dispersed through the light blue Copper (II) Sulfate solution (Fig. 1). After about 20 minutes, the water gradually darkened and became more opaque (Fig. 2), indicating significant copper activity. Once the reaction reached this stage, I carefully transferred the solution into sealed glassware for observation and safety.

Results

I’m extremely excited with the results: a thick, uniform copper layer formed all around the cup. It was also fascinating to observe how the electrical resistance dropped from the raw metal (Fig. 3) to the newly deposited copper layer (Fig. 4). The robust coating demonstrates the effectiveness of the process and provides a strong foundation for future exploration, particularly for projects involving PCBs, homemade motors, or other electrical applications.
